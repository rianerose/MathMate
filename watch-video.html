The level of significance, often denoted by the symbol Î± (alpha), is a parameter used in hypothesis testing to make decisions about rejecting or failing to reject a null hypothesis. It represents the maximum acceptable probability of committing a Type I error, which is the error of rejecting a null hypothesis when it is actually true. The choice between a one-tailed and two-tailed test depends on the research question and the specific hypothesis being tested. It is important to define the alternative hypothesis appropriately to ensure the test aligns with the intended analysis.
