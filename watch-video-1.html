In statistics, the null hypothesis (H0) and the alternative hypothesis (H1 or Ha) are two competing statements about a population parameter or a statistical relationship that we want to investigate. These hypotheses are fundamental components of hypothesis testing, which is a common method used to make inferences and draw conclusions from data.
